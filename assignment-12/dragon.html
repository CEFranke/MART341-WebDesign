What Are Various Draconic Types?



Most individuals don't actually care about the difference between dragon like entities. Large scaly reptile (likely with wings) is more than enough to make people say "Thats a dragon". In reality there is a lot more nuanced definitions, specifically with the names of various draconic types telling you how the little beastie is built.



Dragon

The most common name used is actually a pretty limited definition in terms of limbs it should have. A traditional "Dragon" should be composed of 4 Legs and 2 Wings. Granted the number of wings may be higher, but not lower.



Wyvern

Wyvern is the next most common name to be misused in common media. A traditional "Wyvern" should be composed of 2 Legs and 2 Wings. The wings are allowed to also act as front limbs, but must first and formost be wings.



Long/Lung

Long/Lung is what one sees in the media to be a Asian style Dragon. Long thin bodies. A traditional "Long/Lung" should be composed of 4 Legs. The wings certain depictions of them do have a set of wings, but it is also just as common to have them flying without the use of wings.



Lindwyrm

Lindwyrm is a more uncommon term to be used in media today, but very traditional in old use. A traditional "Lindwyrm" should be composed of 2 Legs. Most commonly seen in a form of having front limbs that can drag the rest of the body forward. In the case of wings, they usually would be tiny and useless for flight.



Drake

Drake is a uncommon term to be used in media. A traditional "Drake" should be composed of 4 Legs and 0 wings. What is usually considered a proper use for the term is something akin to a dragon but merely lacking the wings.



Wyrm

Wyrm is am old common term. One of the main features is a long spindly body, but still with Western 'dragon' style. A traditional "Wyrm" should be composed of 0 Legs or 4 short legs. In the case a Wyrm would have wings, they usually are tiny and not able to be used for flight.



Sea Serpent

Sea Serpent is frankly exactly what is sounds like. Long serpentine bodies built for the water. A traditional "Sea Serpent" should be composed of 0 Legs and 0 "wings". The singlular exception to the limb situation is the presece of fins.



Amphiptere

Amphiptere is rather rare term to hear today. Long serpentine bodies, built for flight. A traditional "Amphiptere" should be composed of 0 Legs and 2 wings. There of course can be more wings, but it must NOT have legs.
